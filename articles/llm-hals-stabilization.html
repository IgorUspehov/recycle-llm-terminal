HALS + carbon black for recycled HDPE outdoor pipe

Application: corrugated drainage pipe from 100% recycled HDPE (mixed post-consumer + post-industrial). Required UV lifetime: minimum 10 years outdoor exposure in Central Europe. Wall thickness 3.5mm.

The command

ihor@recycle-llm:~$ llm "stabilizer package for outdoor HDPE pipe: 100% recycled HDPE mixed post-consumer + post-industrial, 10yr UV lifetime target, Central Europe, wall 3.5mm. Specify: carbon black % and particle size, HALS grade, antioxidant. Any interaction risks."

Model output

Recommended stabilizer package for outdoor recycled HDPE pipe (10yr / CE):

Carbon black:
— Loading: 2.0–2.5% (pipe grade, not film grade)
— Particle size: 20–25nm (furnace black, N330 or equivalent)
— Distribution: critical — CB must be fully dispersed, no agglomerates
— Note: CB below 20nm = better UV absorption but harder dispersion

HALS:
— Primary: Chimassorb 944 (polymeric, low migration, 10yr-grade) 0.3–0.5%
— OR: Tinuvin 622 at 0.4% (lower MW, faster activation, better for regrind)
— For recycled material: Tinuvin 622 preferred — less sensitive to AO antagonism

Antioxidant:
— Primary AO: Irganox 1010, 0.15–0.2%
— Processing stabilizer: Irgafos 168, 0.1%

Interaction risk — CRITICAL:
HALS + acidic pigments or flame retardants = deactivation.
HALS high MW types (944) show antagonism with some phenolic AOs in regrind
with residual acid catalyst — test compatibility at 220°C/10min before scale-up.

Estimated weathering performance at this package: 12–15 yr CE exposure.

My assessment

The Tinuvin 622 preference over Chimassorb 944 for regrind is a nuanced and correct recommendation — 944 can show antagonism with residual acid in recycled material. The CB particle size 20–25nm is correct for balance of UV absorption and dispersion. The HALS/AO antagonism warning is important and often overlooked. I'd run the 622 + 1010 + 168 package as described.

Conclusion

For stabilizer package design, LLM gives commercially realistic recommendations including the interaction risks that textbooks skip. The Tinuvin vs Chimassorb distinction for regrind is something that only comes from experience — the model got it right.
