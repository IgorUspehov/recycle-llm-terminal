MFI drift prediction for LLDPE multilayer regrind

Material: LLDPE/LDPE multilayer stretch film regrind, 3 passes through granulator. Initial MFI: 1.8 g/10min (190°C/2.16kg). Question: how much will MFI shift after 2 more reprocessing cycles?

The command

ihor@recycle-llm:~$ ollama run mistral
>>> LLDPE/LDPE multilayer film regrind, starting MFI 1.8 g/10min (190C/2.16kg), already 3 passes through extruder at 200-215°C. No antioxidant added. Predict MFI after 2 more reprocessing passes. Explain mechanism.

Model output

For LLDPE without antioxidant protection after thermal history of 3 passes:

Dominant mechanism: thermo-oxidative chain scission (MFI increases)
Secondary: minor crosslinking in LDPE fraction (partially offsetting)

Predicted MFI after pass 4: ~2.3–2.5 g/10min (+25–38%)
Predicted MFI after pass 5: ~3.0–3.5 g/10min (+65–95% from original)

Critical warning: above MFI 3.0 the film regrind becomes unsuitable for blown film
blend ratio above 30% without mechanical property compensation.

Recommendation: add 0.1–0.15% Irganox 1076 before pass 4 to limit drift to +15%.

My assessment

Prediction range is realistic. In my practice with similar multilayer LLDPE/LDPE I've seen +20–40% MFI jump per pass without AO, so the model's numbers are in the correct range. The Irganox 1076 recommendation is appropriate — lower volatility than 1010 for film applications. The 30% blend limit note is also practical.

Conclusion

LLM gives a useful ballpark for MFI drift trajectory. Not a substitute for lab measurement, but helps plan when to add stabilizer and at what blend ratio to cap regrind content.
